The Concept

WHAT IS
MOMENT MARKETS?

Moment Markets is a real-time engagement layer that sits on top of existing live sports streams. It detects high-impact moments during a match — a penalty, a free kick, a VAR review — and presents a targeted prediction directly on-screen, which a viewer can act on with a single tap, in under six seconds.

Most people watching a live match on a betting platform never place an in-play bet. Not because they don't want to — but because by the time they find the market and confirm the bet, the moment has passed. Moment Markets removes every step between the impulse and the action.

The Problem

Current in-play betting requires users to leave the stream, navigate market lists, build a bet slip, and confirm. This five-step process kills the impulse that live sport generates. Most live stream viewers are passive. They watch but never bet.

The Opportunity

Betway already has the stream, the wallet, the user base, and the gambling licence. The infrastructure is in place. What is missing is a mechanism to activate the viewing audience at the exact emotional peak of a match — the moment the free kick is awarded, not after.

The Solution

A lightweight overlay appears at key match events. It asks one question. It gives the viewer six to ten seconds to respond. One tap. Settled automatically. No navigation, no bet slip, no friction. The viewer never leaves the stream.

Product Experience

HOW IT WORKS
IN PRACTICE

The experience is designed to feel like a natural part of watching the match — not an interruption. Every design decision is made to reduce friction to as close to zero as possible.

01

The Viewer Watches Live

A user opens the Betway app and begins watching a live Premier League match. They are engaged with the stream but have not interacted with any betting market. This is the majority of live stream users — spectators, not bettors. Under the current model, they generate no revenue during the match.

02

A Key Moment Is Detected

A foul is committed on the edge of the box. The referee signals a free kick. Within milliseconds, the system's event detection engine receives a signal from the live match data feed — the same data feed Betway already subscribes to. The system identifies this as a high-value predictive moment.

03

The Overlay Appears

A semi-transparent overlay slides in at the bottom of the screen. It presents a single question — for example, "Bruno Fernandes: will he score this free kick?" — alongside two clearly labelled options and their respective odds. A countdown timer is visible. The overlay is designed to feel native to the stream, not like an advertisement.

04

One Tap to Predict

The user selects their prediction with a single tap before the timer expires. The stake is drawn instantly from their existing Betway wallet. No confirmation screen. No bet slip. No secondary action required. The overlay disappears immediately after selection.

05

Instant Settlement

The free kick is taken. The outcome — goal or no goal — is confirmed by the data feed. The user receives a brief visual result notification on-screen. Winnings are credited to their wallet in real time. The stream continues uninterrupted. The entire cycle from overlay appearance to settlement lasts under thirty seconds.

Trigger Events

WHEN THE OVERLAY
APPEARS

The system does not activate on every event — only on moments with meaningful predictive value and strong emotional engagement from viewers. Frequency is deliberately limited to preserve the impact of each overlay.

High Priority

Penalty Kick

Binary outcome. High emotional stakes. Clear taker identified. Strong data history per player and goalkeeper combination.

High Priority

Direct Free Kick

Positional context available. Shot-on-target and scoring probability calculable from distance, angle, and player history.

Medium Priority

VAR Review

Extended pause in play. High viewer attention. Binary outcome — will the decision be overturned or upheld.

Medium Priority

Corner Kick

Will the corner result in a shot? In a goal? Probability calculable from team set-piece data and defensive positioning.

Medium Priority

Tactical Substitution

Will the substitute score or assist? Lightweight market with high fan interest, particularly for marquee substitutions.

Controlled

Injury Time

Will there be a late goal? Volatile but high-engagement moment. Limited to late-game scenarios where the scoreline creates genuine tension.

Maximum recommended frequency: 6 to 10 overlays per 90-minute match. Below this threshold, each overlay feels significant rather than routine.

Odds Intelligence

WHAT POWERS
THE PREDICTIONS

The odds displayed in each overlay are not static figures taken from a standard market. They are dynamically calculated in real time using a multi-variable intelligence model. This is the core proprietary component of the system — and the primary point of differentiation from anything currently available in the market.

Standard sportsbook odds for in-play events are updated periodically and reflect broad statistical averages. The Moment Markets model recalculates continuously, incorporating conditions that are specific to the current moment in the match — not just historical averages.

Variable 01

In-Match Fatigue Index

Every outfield player accumulates a live fatigue score throughout the match. This is calculated from minutes played, total distance covered, sprint frequency in the preceding five minutes, and recovery time between high-intensity efforts. A player taking a free kick in the 84th minute after a physically demanding game has meaningfully different execution probability than the same player in the 22nd minute.

Fatigue does not mean a player becomes bad. It means their precision and power output degrade in measurable, predictable ways.

Variable 02

Psychological Pressure Index

Some players perform statistically better under pressure. Others do not. The pressure index incorporates the current scoreline, match importance (league position implications, knockout stage, derby), home or away status, crowd noise intensity where available, and the player's performance record in comparable high-pressure moments.

This variable is deliberately underweighted in standard market pricing, creating a genuine informational edge.

Variable 03

Goalkeeper Bias Metrics

For penalties and direct free kicks, the defending goalkeeper is as important a variable as the taker. The model incorporates historical dive direction tendencies, reaction time data, positioning drift under pressure, and performance record in specific scenario types. Some goalkeepers show strong left-bias when diving; others show inconsistency that can be modelled.

Variable 04

Match Momentum Index

Football is a sport defined by streaks and momentum. The momentum index measures possession percentage in the preceding ten minutes, territory control, recent shot conversion, and the flow of referee decisions. A team that has been dominant for twenty minutes and just won a free kick is in a meaningfully different position than one that has been under sustained defensive pressure.

Variable 05

Environmental Conditions

Wind speed and direction, pitch wetness, ambient temperature, and stadium altitude all have documented effects on ball trajectory, player performance, and shot accuracy. These variables are incorporated from live weather feeds and are particularly relevant for free kicks, where trajectory prediction is central to the outcome.

Most betting operators ignore environmental data entirely. Its inclusion is a meaningful differentiator.

Variable 06

Defensive Disruption Score

For set pieces, the defending wall's consistency matters. The model incorporates jump timing inconsistency in the wall, recent fouls conceded near the box, and the goalkeeper's communication patterns with defenders. A poorly organised wall with a goalkeeper who has shown positional inconsistency changes the probability calculation significantly.

Standard sportsbooks use historical averages. Moment Markets uses the present. That gap — between what a player has done on average and what they are likely to do right now, at this moment, under these conditions — is where the intelligence lives.

Technical Architecture

HOW THE SYSTEM
IS BUILT

The following is a high-level description of the system architecture, written for a non-technical audience. Detailed engineering specifications are available in the supplementary technical annex upon request.

COMPONENT A

Event Detection Engine

This component listens in real time to the official match data feed — the same feed used by Betway and every major sportsbook — and identifies when a trigger event has occurred. It converts the raw data signal into an internal command to activate the overlay system.

Data providers: Opta, Sportradar, Genius Sports. All are existing Betway suppliers.

COMPONENT B

Prediction Engine

Upon event detection, the prediction engine calculates a real-time probability for each possible outcome. It ingests live player data, environmental feeds, and match context, and applies the multi-variable intelligence model described above. Output is a set of odds displayed in the overlay.

Model type: gradient boosting with real-time Bayesian updating.

COMPONENT C

Latency Synchronisation Layer

This is the most technically critical component. Live streams typically run with a five to fifteen second delay from real-world events. The overlay must appear before the event executes on the viewer's screen, and bets must lock before the outcome is visible. The synchronisation layer calibrates the overlay trigger timing against the viewer's specific stream delay.

A two-second safety buffer is applied before outcome resolution.

COMPONENT D

Overlay Delivery System

A lightweight software layer injected into the streaming client — the Betway mobile app or web player — that renders the overlay interface at the correct moment. It is designed to be visually unobtrusive, dismissible, and fast-rendering on low-bandwidth connections. The interface is rendered client-side to minimise delivery latency.

COMPONENT E

Wallet Integration

Bet placement and settlement connect directly to Betway's existing wallet infrastructure via API. No new payment system is required. The overlay system creates a micro-market, places the bet on the user's behalf upon tap confirmation, and triggers settlement when the outcome is confirmed by the data feed. The entire financial flow operates within Betway's existing licensed environment.

ANTI-EXPLOITATION

Lockout and Safety Mechanisms

The system applies a hard bet lockout window in the final moments before the event executes — preventing any user from placing a bet once they can see the outcome on their screen. An anti-arbitrage timing lock prevents systematic exploitation of stream delay differences. These mechanisms are built into the latency synchronisation layer.

Secondary Revenue

BEYOND THE
MICRO-BET

Direct betting turnover is the primary revenue driver, but the overlay system creates additional monetisation pathways that compound its value over time.

Sponsored Overlays

Brand sponsors — particularly in automotive, FMCG, and financial services — can co-brand high-profile overlay moments. "This free kick is brought to you by…" creates a premium, high-attention advertising inventory that does not exist on any current platform. Priced per activation, not per impression.

Premium Analytics Tier

A visible data layer accompanying the overlay — showing the fatigue index, momentum index, and historical tendencies — can be unlocked as a premium subscription add-on. Sports bettors who value data will pay for visibility into the model powering their decisions.

Data Licensing

The intelligence engine generates a proprietary dataset: real-time player performance metrics tied to betting outcomes, at scale. This dataset has value beyond the SA market and can be licensed to other operators, broadcasters, or sports analytics firms in subsequent phases.

Responsible Gambling

SAFEGUARDS AND
ETHICAL DESIGN

The design of this system incorporates responsible gambling principles from the ground up, not as an afterthought. These are not just ethical considerations — they are regulatory requirements and commercial necessities.

Frequency Caps

Individual users can be limited in the number of overlay interactions per match or per session. Frequency limits prevent the system from becoming a high-speed gambling loop and protect the product from regulatory challenge on harm grounds.

Spend Limits

Micro-bet stake sizes are capped per overlay event. The psychological framing of small, fast bets requires strong default limits to prevent cumulative spend exceeding what a user would consciously authorise in a single bet.

Clear Opt-Out

Every overlay is dismissible with a single swipe. Users can disable the overlay feature entirely in settings. The experience must never feel forced or inescapable. Opt-out visibility is a regulatory expectation and a UX necessity.

Implementation

PROPOSED
ROLLOUT PLAN

A phased implementation reduces risk, creates measurable proof points at each stage, and allows the system to be refined before full commercial deployment.

Phase 01 — Months 1 to 2
Feasibility and Architecture

Technical feasibility confirmed through latency benchmarking on Betway's existing stream infrastructure. Data feed integration scoped with the existing Opta or Sportradar provider. Initial prediction model built and tested against historical match data. UX prototype designed and user-tested internally. Regulatory pre-consultation with relevant provincial gambling board initiated.

Phase 02 — Months 3 to 5
Pilot — Free Credit Mode

Live pilot launched on a selection of Premier League matches, restricted to free-credit engagement. Real money is not wagered. The pilot measures overlay interaction rate, session duration change, user sentiment, and technical performance under live conditions. A/B test against control group of equivalent stream viewers who do not receive overlays. All technical failure modes stress-tested in real conditions.

Phase 03 — Months 6 to 8
Real-Money Activation

Subject to regulatory sign-off and pilot performance data, real-money micro-bet activation is introduced. Initially limited to low stake caps. Performance data from Phase 02 is used to optimise event selection logic, overlay frequency, and odds model accuracy. Revenue reporting framework established for ongoing commercial monitoring.

Phase 04 — Month 9 onwards
Full Deployment and Expansion

Full rollout across all streamed Premier League matches. Expansion to additional leagues — Champions League, local PSL fixtures, other sports including cricket and rugby as applicable. Sponsored overlay programme launched. Premium analytics tier introduced. Personalisation layer built to refine which overlays are presented to which users based on betting history and engagement patterns.

The Strategic Opportunity

THE WINDOW
IS OPEN

The convergence of live sports streaming and in-play betting is not a new observation. What remains unsolved, globally, is the last-mile problem: connecting the peak emotional moment of a live match to a betting action without interrupting the experience.

Betway is positioned to solve this first in the South African market. The infrastructure exists. The user base exists. The data feeds exist. The licence exists. What is required is the intelligence layer and the overlay system — a development investment that, at conservative projections, recovers its cost within a single Premier League season.

The competitive risk is real. Genius Sports, through BetVision, is moving in this direction globally. The question is not whether this product will exist in the market — it will. The question is whether Betway builds it, or whether it is delivered to them by a third-party vendor on the vendor's terms.

This is not a feature. It is an engagement infrastructure that compounds in value the longer it operates — building proprietary data, user behavioural profiles, and a product experience that no competitor can replicate without building from scratch.
